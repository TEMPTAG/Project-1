Let's Start Cooking Birria Beef!

Prepare the Chuck Roast:

1. Cut the Chuck Roast into 4" cubes, pat dry, and lightly coat in Olive Oil


2. Season the Chuck Roast with Salt and Pepper


3. Heat Olive Oil in a Large Dutch Oven, and sear all sides of the seasoned Chuck Roast on medium-high heat


4. Remove the Dutch Oven from the heat keeping the browned meat remnants in the bowl, and set the Chuck Roast aside in a bowl


Prepare the Marinade:

1. Rinse, stem, and seed the Guajillo, Ancho, and √Årbol chiles, and add them to a medium-sized pot


2. Slice the Roma Tomatoes in half, remove the stems, and add them to the pot


3. Peel and slice the Yellow Onion into 1/2" slivers, then add to the pot


4. Add the Mexican Cinnamon Sticks, Bay Leaves, and Peppercorns to the pot


5. Cover the ingredients with water and bring to a boil over high heat


6. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let the pot simmer for 10min


7. fter 10min, using a slotted spoon, transfer the ingredients to a blender or food processor (SAVE THE WATER!)


8. Add 1 cup of the chili water, the Beef Broth, White Vinegar, Garlic, Cumin, Oregano, and Ground Cloves to the blender or food processor, and blend until very smooth


9. Using a fine-mesh strainer, strain the blended sauce into the Dutch Oven, discard any solids from the sauce


Cooking the chuck roast:

1.Take the Dutch Oven with the marinade and add the Chuck Roast back in


2.Bring the Chuck Roast and marinade to a boil over high heat


3. Reduce to low heat, cover, and cook for 3-3 1/2 hours until the Chuck Roast is fall-apart tender


4. Remove the Chuck Roast, shred, and return to the Dutch Oven


Serve as a stew with cilantro and white onions, or in tacos, burritos, etc.
