Let's Start Cooking Al Pastor

Prepare the Pork:

  1. 1. Pound the Pork slices so they are even thickness.
  2. 2. Place them in the refrigerator until it is time to cook.

Prepare the marinade:

  1. 1. In a pan over medium heat, lightly brown the onions and garlic in olive oil.
  2. 2. Once lightly browned, add the seeded and stemmed guajillo chiles and bay leaves. Saute for 1-2 minutes.
  3. 3. Add 1/4 of water, and the 1/4 cup of Apple Cider Vinegar.
  4. 4. Cover on low heat for 10 minutes, then turn off heat and let it cool.
  5. 5. Pour contents of the pan into the blender with the spices (Salt, Cumin, Oregano, Black Pepper, and All Spice), Chipotle Chiles, Chicken Stock, and Pineapple Juice, and blend until very smooth.
  6. 6. Pour the contents of the blender through a fine-mesh strainer into a large bowl. Push the blended marinade through the strainer until no liquid remains. Discard the solids.
  7. 7. Mix the Pork slices into the large bowl of marinade until they are coated. Cover and put back into the refrigerator to marinate for 4-24 hours.

Cook the Pork:

  1. 1. Preheat your bbq grill to as hot as possible.
  2. 2. Cook the Pork on the grill until an internal temperature of 160 degrees
  3. 3. Slice and cook Pineapple slices on the grill alongside the Pork for best flavor
  4. 4. Once cooked fully, remove the Pork from the Grill and let it rest for a minimum of 10 minute
  5. 5. Dice the Pork and Pineapple. Serve in tacos, burritos, or any of your other favorite meals.
