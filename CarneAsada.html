Let's Start Cooking Carne Asada!

Prepare the marinade:

1. Preheat the broiler, place the Jalepeños on a baking sheet and broil until skin begins to turn black, then flip and repeat on the other side


2.Stem, seed, and skin the Jalapeños into a blender


3. Core and quarter the Tomato, and add to the blender


4. Peel and quarter the Onion, and add 3 of the quarters into the blender


5. Peel the Garlic and add to the blender


6. Juice the Orange, and add the juice to the blender - save the rind of the orange


7. Juice 2 of the Limes, and add the juice to the blender


8. Rinse the cilantro and cut the bottom 2" off of the stems, add half of the bunch to the blender


9.Finally, add the Ancho Chili Powder, Sugar, Salt, Black Pepper, Mirin, Avocado Oil and blend until smooth


Marinate the Arrachera:

1. Add the Arrachera to a large bowl or zip lock bag (might take a couple of bags)


2. Slice half of the Orange Rind and the last 1/4 of the Onion into 1/4" slices and add to the Marinade


3. Mix the Corona with the Marinade.


4. Mix the meat in the marinade, seal, and let it marinate in the refrigerator for 24 hours.


5. Flip the meat in the marinade halfway through


Cook the Marinated Arrachera

1.Put your grill on as high of heat as possible.


2. Lightly oil the grill with some Avocado oil


3. Cook the Arrachera until desired temperature


4. Let stand for 10 minutes after removing from the grill and slice against the grain


5. Serve with sliced Lime wedges from the remaining lime, and diced Cilantro
